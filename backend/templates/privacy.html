InstructionsCrafter — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-01

What we collect

When you use the InstructionsCrafter Chrome extension:

  • Screen recording and microphone audio captured during a recording session, plus the timing of your mouse clicks and the on-screen labels of the controls you click (used to place each screenshot at the right moment).
  • The document title you enter on the post-recording page.
  • Local settings stored in chrome.storage.local: the backend URL, an optional default document title, recording session state, and the shared password (saved after a successful generation so you do not have to retype it, and cleared automatically if the server rejects it). Chrome clears all of this when you uninstall the extension.

We do not collect: your name, email, IP address, browser history, cookies, screen content outside of an active recording session, or any data unrelated to producing the requested document.

Where the data goes

Recordings and titles are sent to our own backend server (default: https://instructionscrafter.com) over HTTPS. The backend pipeline:

  1. Saves the upload to a per-request temporary directory.
  2. Sends the entire recording — both the video and the audio — to Google's Gemini API, which watches it and returns the document's introduction, the step-by-step instructions, and the moment to capture for each screenshot.
  3. Extracts the screenshots locally with FFmpeg and assembles them with the text into a .docx file.
  4. Returns the .docx file to your browser.
  5. Deletes the upload and every intermediate file from our server as soon as the response is sent, and deletes the copy uploaded to Google as soon as Gemini finishes processing it.

No copy is retained on our server. No database persists user content.

Third-party services

  • Google (Gemini API) — receives the entire recording (video and audio) in order to generate the document. InstructionsCrafter uses a paid Gemini API tier, on which Google does not use the content you submit to train or improve its models. Subject to Google's Gemini API Additional Terms of Service and the Google Privacy Policy.

Google is the only third party that receives your recording.

Authentication

The extension sends a shared password (entered on the post-recording page) with every request. The backend verifies it and rejects unauthenticated requests. After a successful generation the password is saved in chrome.storage.local so you do not have to retype it on later visits; it is cleared automatically if the server rejects it, and Chrome removes it when you uninstall the extension. It is never sent anywhere except to our own backend.

Data sharing

We do not share, sell, or transfer user data beyond the third-party service listed above.
